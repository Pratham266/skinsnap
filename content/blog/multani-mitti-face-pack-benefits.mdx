Multani mitti, also called Fuller's earth, has been sitting in Indian homes for generations, long before fancy clay masks arrived in stores. Your grandmother probably swore by it, and honestly, she was not wrong.

But there is a right way and a wrong way to use it. Used well, it is one of the most effective natural options for oily and combination skin. Used wrong, it can leave your face dry, tight, and unhappy. This guide covers both sides.

## What Are the Benefits of Multani Mitti for Your Face?

Multani mitti is a natural clay rich in minerals like magnesium and silica. Its biggest talent is absorption: it soaks up excess oil, sweat, and grime from the surface of your skin like a sponge.

### Oil control that you can actually feel

If your T-zone turns shiny by afternoon, this is where multani mitti shines. It pulls out extra sebum from the skin surface, so your face stays matte for longer. This makes it especially popular in humid Indian summers when oily skin works overtime.

### Deep cleansing of pores

As the pack dries, it gently draws out dirt and buildup sitting inside your pores. Skin often feels noticeably cleaner and smoother after a single use. Over a few weeks of regular use, many people notice fewer blackheads and less congestion around the nose.

### A mild, natural exfoliating effect

When you rinse the pack off with gentle circular motions, the fine clay particles lift away dead skin cells. This is much softer than harsh scrubs, which can create tiny scratches on the skin.

### Calming for heat and mild irritation

Multani mitti has a naturally cooling feel on the skin. That is why it is a favourite after long days out in the sun, and why it pairs so well with tan care. If sun exposure is your bigger concern, read our guide on [how to remove tan at home](/blog/how-to-remove-tan-at-home).

## How to Use Multani Mitti on Your Face?

The method matters as much as the ingredient. Here is a simple routine that works.

### Step 1: Start with a clean face

Wash your face with a mild cleanser and pat it dry. Applying clay over makeup or sunscreen just traps that layer against your skin.

### Step 2: Mix a fresh, smooth paste

Take about two spoons of multani mitti powder and mix it with rose water until you get a paste like thick curd. Not watery, not crumbly. Rose water is the classic partner here because it soothes while the clay cleans — we cover why in our post on [rose water for face](/blog/rose-water-for-face).

### Step 3: Apply a medium layer

Spread an even layer over your face, avoiding the eye area and lips. A paper-thin layer dries too fast; a very thick layer never dries. Aim for something in between.

### Step 4: Rinse before it fully cracks

This is the step most people get wrong. Wash the pack off with plain water when it is semi-dry, around 10 to 15 minutes in. If the clay has turned pale and cracked everywhere, you have waited too long.

### Step 5: Always moisturise after

Clay removes oil, so give some hydration back. A light moisturiser keeps the balance right and prevents the tight, stretched feeling.

## How Often Should You Use a Multani Mitti Pack?

Two to three times a week is plenty for oily skin. Once a week suits normal and combination skin. If your skin is dry or sensitive, use it occasionally on the oily areas only, or skip it in dry winter months. ## Mistakes to Avoid with Multani Mitti

A few plain warnings, because this ingredient gets over-hyped online.

First, do not expect it to erase dark spots or scars. It cleans and controls oil; it does not change your skin's pigment. Second, avoid mixing it with lemon juice directly on the face — lemon is acidic and can sting or irritate skin, especially before sun exposure. Third, do not store leftover wet paste for the next day. Once mixed with liquid, it starts losing freshness and can pick up germs.

Also remember that results are gradual. You will feel cleaner skin from day one, but visible improvement in texture and breakouts takes a few weeks of consistent use.

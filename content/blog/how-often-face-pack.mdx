Face packs are one of those skincare habits where more is not better. Use one too rarely and you barely see results. Use one too often and you can leave your skin dry, tight, and irritated.

So what is the sweet spot? It depends on your skin type and the kind of pack you use. This guide gives you clear numbers, and then covers five common face pack mistakes that quietly undo your results.

## How Often Should You Use a Face Pack by Skin Type?

For clay-based packs like multani mitti, here is a simple frequency guide you can actually follow.

### Oily and acne-prone skin: 2 to 3 times a week

Oily skin benefits the most from clay packs because they absorb excess sebum and clean out congested pores. Two to three sessions a week keeps oil in check without stripping the skin. If your face feels tight or flaky, drop to twice a week.

### Combination skin: 1 to 2 times a week

With combination skin, you can either use a pack once or twice a week on the full face, or apply it only on the oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) and skip the drier cheeks. This targeted approach is underrated and works very well.

### Normal skin: once a week

If your skin is neither too oily nor too dry, a weekly pack is enough to keep it fresh and clean. More than that adds no real benefit.

### Dry or sensitive skin: once a week or less

Clay is naturally absorbent, so dry skin needs caution. Once a week at most, kept on for a shorter time (8 to 10 minutes), and always followed by moisturiser. If your skin stings or turns very red, take a longer break between uses.

One more thing: results come from consistency over weeks, not intensity in one weekend. A pack once or twice a week for two months will do far more than daily packs for one week.

## What Are the 5 Most Common Face Pack Mistakes?

Frequency is only half the story. These five mistakes are the reason many people say face packs "do not work" for them.

### Mistake 1: Letting the pack dry until it cracks

That fully cracked, statue-like finish feels satisfying, but it is the stage where the clay starts pulling moisture out of your skin instead of oil and dirt. Rinse when the pack is mostly dry but still slightly tacky, usually around 10 to 15 minutes.

### Mistake 2: Applying on unwashed skin

A face pack applied over sweat, sunscreen, and the day's grime cannot reach your pores properly. Always cleanse first, then apply the pack on clean, slightly damp skin. The pack works better and rinses off easier.

### Mistake 3: Using stale or contaminated paste

Mixing a big batch and storing the leftover for tomorrow is a hygiene problem. A wet, preservative-free paste is a perfect home for bacteria within hours. The same goes for scooping powder from a jar with wet fingers or a wet spoon. ### Mistake 4: Skipping moisturiser afterwards

A clay pack removes oil, including some oil your skin actually needs. Rinsing off and walking away leaves the skin dry and unprotected, and many people find their skin feels oilier or more irritated afterwards. A light moisturiser after every pack keeps things balanced. This one habit fixes most "my skin felt worse after" complaints.

### Mistake 5: Expecting overnight miracles

No face pack removes acne in one use or transforms skin tone in a weekend. Realistic expectations look like this: skin feels cleaner and less oily immediately, looks a bit fresher after a few uses, and shows steadier improvement over four to eight weeks of regular use. Anything promising more than that is marketing, not skincare.

## Does the Type of Face Pack Change the Frequency?

Yes, a little. Strong oil-absorbing packs (multani mitti) sit at the stricter end of the frequency guide above. Gentler brightening packs, like [orange peel powder for skin](/blog/orange-peel-powder-for-skin), are milder and easier to tolerate at the same frequency.

If you rotate different packs, count them together. Two multani mitti sessions plus two orange peel sessions in the same week is still four packs, which is too many for most skin. Keep the weekly total within your skin type's limit, whichever packs you choose.

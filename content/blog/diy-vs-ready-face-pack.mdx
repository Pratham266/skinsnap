Every Indian household has an opinion on this. One side swears by the kitchen: multani mitti from the local store, besan, haldi, and dadi's exact ratios. The other side wants a tube or pouch that just works.

Most articles on this topic are secretly ads for one side. This one is not. Here is a fair comparison of homemade vs readymade face packs, so you can pick what suits your life.

## What Makes a DIY Multani Mitti Face Pack So Popular?

Let us give DIY its full due, because it has real advantages.

### It is very cheap per use

A 100g packet of loose multani mitti costs a fraction of any branded product and yields many applications. If you use face packs regularly and buy in bulk, nothing beats DIY on cost per use. This is the single biggest reason it remains popular, and it is a completely valid one.

### It is fully customisable

Your skin, your recipe. Extra oily this week? Add a little more clay. Feeling dull? Add a pinch of orange peel powder. Want a soothing version? More rose water, shorter time. DIY gives you control that no fixed formula can match, and experienced users genuinely benefit from this flexibility.

### You know what is going in

When you mix it yourself from ingredients you trust, there are no hidden preservatives, fragrances, or fillers. For people who react to unknown ingredients, this transparency is valuable.

A simple, reliable DIY recipe: two spoons of multani mitti, enough rose water to form a smooth curd-like paste, applied for 10 to 15 minutes on clean skin. That is it. Skip the lemon juice that many recipes suggest; it is acidic and harsher on skin than most people realise.

## Where Does DIY Quietly Fail?

Now the other half. DIY's problems are not about the recipe. They are about everything around the recipe.

### Storage ruins bulk ingredients

Clay powders are hygroscopic, meaning they absorb moisture from the air. That open packet of multani mitti in a humid Indian bathroom slowly clumps, hardens, and degrades. One wet spoon dipped into the jar can introduce bacteria that grow quietly over weeks. We wrote a full guide on this in [how to store multani mitti](/blog/how-to-store-multani-mitti), because it is the most ignored part of DIY skincare.

### Dosing is guesswork

"Two spoons" of powder and "some" rose water gives you a different paste every time: sometimes too runny, sometimes too thick, often more than you need. The leftover gets stored "for tomorrow", which is a hygiene problem, or thrown away, which quietly eats into that cost advantage.

### The mess and effort add up

A bowl, a spoon, measuring, mixing, and cleaning up afterwards. It sounds small, but it is exactly why so many jars of multani mitti sit unused after the first month. A face pack that is too much effort is a face pack you skip.

## Are Ready-Made Face Packs Actually Better?

Ready-made packs solve the problems above, but the category has its own trap, so let us be precise.

### Where ready-made formats win

Consistency and dosing: the ratio is fixed by the maker, so every application behaves the same. Hygiene: a sealed product has never met your bathroom's humidity or a wet spoon. Convenience and travel: no bowl, no mixing station, no cleanup. For busy people, this is the difference between using a pack weekly and not using it at all.

### Where you must read the label

Many mainstream cream-based packs stay soft in the tube for two years because of preservatives, and some add fragrance and colourants. That is not automatically dangerous, but it is a real trade-off against the DIY promise of pure ingredients. If you buy ready-made, check the ingredient list, not just the front of the pack.

So the real question is not homemade vs readymade. It is: can you get DIY-level purity with ready-made convenience?

## DIY vs Ready-Made: The Quick Verdict

Choose DIY if you use packs very frequently, enjoy the ritual, want full control over recipes, and are disciplined about airtight storage and dry spoons. It is the cheapest path and a perfectly good one.

Choose ready-made if you want consistent results without effort, travel often, share a humid bathroom, or know from experience that a mixing-bowl routine will not survive your schedule. Also lean ready-made if past DIY attempts ended with hardened powder in a forgotten jar.

Whichever you choose, the fundamentals stay the same: clean skin before, 10 to 15 minutes on, moisturiser after, and sensible frequency.
